From a Front End perspective, using AgileWiki makes a lot of sense. The framework manages to deliver on several fronts. It comes with a powerful XML based templating language. Which although requiring a bit of mind shift, is terse and powerful and promises a certain degree of control over the backend while leaving the usual paradigms of separation intact.

I developed this site with a very early alpha of AgileWiki5 and immediatley I began to enjoy myself. It is great to be able to use XML fragments and have the ability to easily pass data from one to another - features that mean that there is very little duplication of markup - if any. I also found myself adding pages to the site very easily.

I've yet to see what a Web Designer will think of structure of a typical AW5 application but I suspect the onus will be on the Front End Developer to make that as clear as possible. I can't imagine it being any more complex than many of the other frameworks out there, in fact I can imagine in may ways it would be simpler.
